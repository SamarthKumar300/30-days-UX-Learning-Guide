Journey Story

Write the full user journey of any app as a story with emotions — not a numbered list of actions, but a narrative with emotional beats at every transition.

Day 1 Week 1
The Notification Brief
SHORT

Respond to a PM's vague brief ("add a notification feature") using the business language framework. Turn ambiguity into a clear scope with metric targets.

Day 2 Week 1
Metric Paragraphs
SHORT

Write how 3 specific design decisions in your ESOP project affect business metrics — using the "I chose X because Y, supported by Z, which should move metric M" structure.

Day 3 Week 1
5-Second Test
SHORT

Test one screen with 3 people — 5 seconds of exposure, then ask what they remember. Synthesize the results: what communicated clearly, what didn't, and what you would change.

Day 15 Week 3
Business Language Cheat Sheet
SHORT

10 design phrases translated into business language. For each, write the designer version and the PM/exec version — showing the same insight in the language that gets budget approved.

Day 17 Week 3
Micro Case Study
SHORT

One decision story from your ESOP project in 150 words — with a hook that stops the scroll, what you chose NOT to do and why, and the expected outcome or lesson.

Day 22 Week 4
Scan Layer Build
SHORT

Build the 10-second scan layer for your ESOP project: outcome-focused title, 2-sentence summary, one compelling visual hook, and one data point that makes the work feel real.

Day 24 Week 4
Pushback Response
SHORT

Write the full verbal response to a CTO's concern about your ESOP redesign looking "too different." Use the pushback formula: respect + evidence + forward path + specific next step.

Day 25 Week 4
LinkedIn Post
SHORT

One complete micro case study LinkedIn post, ready to publish. Hook line that creates curiosity, 200–250 word decision story, closing question. Must not open with "I am a UX designer."

Day 27 Week 4
Lean Research Plan
SHORT

Design a 3-day research sprint for the ESOP project using the 5 lean research methods — specify the method, participant count, script, and how you'll analyse the findings.

Day 15 Week 3

MEDIUM Medium Assignments 4–6 hours each

Dual User Emotion Map
MEDIUM

Map two different users through the same product flow, identifying their emotional state at each step. Find the divergence point — where they have the same action but completely different feelings.

Day 4 Week 1
PM Brief Sprint Plan
MEDIUM

Build a full 2-day sprint plan for a specific PM request — with scoping questions, timeline, deliverables, and what you'll skip. Show how scale determines process depth.

Day 6 Week 1
Root Cause Analysis
MEDIUM

Apply the 3-why technique to 3 past design decisions you've made. For each, trace from the surface symptom to the actual root cause — then identify how the design should have been different.

Day 9 Week 2
Emotion-First Ideation
MEDIUM

Generate 12 ideas across one product flow — 2 ideas per emotion (frustration, anxiety, confusion, relief, delight, trust). Ideas must directly address the emotion, not just improve the screen.

Day 10 Week 2
Diverge and Converge
MEDIUM

Generate 12 structural ideas for one ESOP flow. Apply 3 real constraints (timeline, engineering limits, user research). Converge to 2 strongest ideas with documented rationale for each cut.

Day 11 Week 2
Story-First IA
MEDIUM

Write the complete narrative of a user's grant management journey. Then derive the information architecture from the story — not from a feature list. Show how the story determines navigation structure.

Day 13 Week 2
Lean Research Sprint Design
MEDIUM

Design a complete research plan with right/wrong validation criteria — including participant recruitment strategy, test script, and the specific signal that would prove your hypothesis false.

Day 16 Week 3
Rationale Formula Practice
MEDIUM

Write 5 design decisions with the full rationale formula: I chose X because [user need], supported by [evidence]. The alternative was Y, but [trade-off]. The expected outcome is [metric impact].

Day 18 Week 3
Scale Planning — 4 Scales
MEDIUM

Take one feature brief and plan it at 4 different time scales: 1-day sprint, 1-week sprint, 1-month project, 2-3 month project. Show what you include and skip at each scale, and why.

Day 19 Week 3
Decision Story Rewrite
MEDIUM

Rewrite the opening section of your ESOP case study in decision story format. Remove all process language. Replace with 3 decision stories. Test: can someone understand the project, hard parts, and your thinking without prior context?

Day 23 Week 4
Verbal Case Study Script
MEDIUM

Write all three versions of your verbal case study: 60-second (hook + one decision + outcome), 5-minute (add 2 decisions + reflection), and what you'd add at 15 minutes. Each version builds — it doesn't repeat.
